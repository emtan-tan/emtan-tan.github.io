What is a Modifier?

A modifier is an addition or change to a standard drink order that allows customers to customize their beverages according to their preferences. Modifiers can include things like extra shots of espresso, different types of milk, syrups, and toppings. They are used to enhance the flavor, texture, and overall experience of the drink. Modifiers can be added to a variety of drinks, including coffee, tea, and blended beverages. By using modifiers, customers can create unique and personalized drinks that suit their tastes and dietary needs.

Here is a general modifier list, not exhaustive but a very short outline:

Some of these can be used across sections but they are laid out in separate categories for easier understanding.

* means you can use prefixes like "no", "extra", or "light" for said modifiers

Hot Drink Modifiers
  • *Whipped cream
  • *Type of Milk (Can be used if adding a splash of milk to a non-milk based beverage)
  • *Foam
  • Stirred
  • Extra Hot or Specify Temperature
Iced Drink Modifiers
  • *Ice Amount (Light ice, extra ice, no ice)
  • *Cold Foam, available in various flavors
  • Scoops of Flavor (Matcha powder, lavender powder, etc.)
  • Inclusion Amounts (i.e. Strawberry pieces, dragonfruit pieces, popping pearls)
